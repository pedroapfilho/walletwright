This is the history behind the library, kept in the docs so the project carries its own context.

## The original goal

Build a minimal Vite dapp that connects a wallet, signs a message, and shows the signature, then test
it with Synpress and Playwright across MetaMask (EVM) and Phantom (EVM and Solana). That exploration
happened in a sibling project, `test-synpress`: a Vite harness, a forked Synpress, and a Docker-based
Linux reproduction. walletwright is the clean-room result of that work.

## What went wrong with Synpress

`@synthetixio/synpress@4.1.2` is the latest published release, and it does not work out of the box:

- Its own E2E CI is red at the `release: 4.1.2` commit. Both the MetaMask and Phantom headful jobs
  fail on `ubuntu-latest` with `xvfb`; only the unit tests pass.
- It was reproduced on Linux with Synpress's exact pinned stack (`playwright-core` 1.48.2,
  Chromium 1140, MetaMask 13.13.1) in the official Playwright container: the Phantom download URL is
  dead, and `connectToDapp()` fails with "Notification page did not appear". The connect flow is
  broken everywhere, including CI.
- Open bug reports from other users (Ubuntu, macOS Sonoma and Sequoia) match the failures: #1310
  (stale connect selector), #1308 and #1307 (stuck on "wallet is ready"), #1290 and #1283 (Chromium
  version mismatch), #1303 (MV3), and more.
- The repo has had no meaningful commits in months. It is effectively unmaintained.

The full, itemized evidence (11 universal issues plus 4 that appear on current deps, with file paths,
matching issue numbers, and the reproduction commands) is in [Synpress issues](/synpress-issues).

## The options considered

1. Keep patching the Synpress fork and pin everything to its old stack.
2. Maintain the fork long-term as the project's wallet-testing dependency.
3. Drop Synpress and build a small, focused library on plain Playwright, keeping the ideas that worked
   and dropping the fork and the version pinning.

We took option 3. The fork (branch `fix/synpress-4.1.2-compat`, 15 files changed) proved the fixes
worked: MetaMask 13.35.1 and Phantom connected and signed, headed, on Playwright 1.61 and
Chromium 1228. But it meant forking an idle upstream and overriding dependencies. walletwright
reimplements those fixes as about ten small modules with no patched dependencies and current wallet
and Chromium versions.

## What carried over

The hard-won behaviors are documented in [How it works](/how-it-works). The short version: run headed;
derive the extension id from the manifest `key` or the load path instead of querying the browser;
navigate to the onboarding page with a retry; poll `context.pages()` for the popup; patch MetaMask's
`completedOnboarding` into LevelDB; use a fresh seed for Phantom and Slush; pick the right
confirm-button selectors per wallet; and extract with `adm-zip`.

## Status

`pnpm build`, `lint`, `typecheck`, and `test` all pass. The `apps/demo` E2E connects and signs on
MetaMask (EVM), Phantom (EVM and Solana), and Slush (Sui) through walletwright's own fixtures.

## Timeline

- **2026-06-17**: Built the `test-synpress` Vite harness and forked Synpress with Playwright 1.61 and
  MV3 fixes. Hit a chromium-1228 service-worker port disconnect, changed approach, then got connect
  and sign working (MetaMask, then Phantom EVM and SVM onboarding).
- **2026-06-18**: Documented the 15 Synpress issues and confirmed they're universal (red Synpress CI,
  a Linux Docker reproduction, and matching upstream issues). Dropped Synpress and built walletwright
  from the `acme-package` turborepo template. Then added ecosystem-aware support and verified Slush
  (Sui) end-to-end.
